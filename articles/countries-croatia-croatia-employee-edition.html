The company now employs over 1,300 talented engineers, designers, and manufacturing specialists across its Croatian headquarters and international facilities. Founded by Mate Rimac in 2009 as a one-person operation focused on electric vehicle conversions, the company has scaled to become a globally recognized manufacturer of high-performance electric hypercars and advanced propulsion technology. The workforce expansion reflects growing international demand for Rimac's products and services, as well as the company's investments in research and development infrastructure. The Nevera R represents the pinnacle of Rimac's engineering capabilities and automotive innovation strategy. This all-electric hypercar produces 2,107 horsepower, making it one of the most powerful production vehicles in the world. The Nevera R combines extreme performance specifications with sophisticated engineering solutions for battery management, thermal control, and aerodynamic efficiency. Limited production volumes (fewer than 150 units globally) maintain exclusivity while generating significant revenue and global publicity. The vehicle's development required breakthrough innovations in electric motor design, energy density management, and high-voltage electrical systems. Each Nevera R commands a purchase price exceeding EUR 2.4 million, positioning it in the ultra-luxury segment alongside competing hypercars from established manufacturers. Beyond the Nevera R, Rimac is advancing autonomous vehicle technology through the Verne project. Verne represents a next-generation robotaxi platform designed for shared mobility services in urban environments. The platform integrates Rimac's electric propulsion expertise with autonomous driving software systems, sensor arrays, and connectivity features. Verne aims to address urban transportation challenges through efficient, zero-emission shared mobility rather than individual vehicle ownership. The project reflects industry trends toward autonomous and shared mobility services as primary urban transportation solutions replacing traditional private car ownership. Rimac's growth trajectory has been accelerated through strategic partnerships with established automotive manufacturers. Porsche holds a significant ownership stake in Rimac, valuing the company's battery and motor technology expertise. Hyundai has invested in Rimac and utilizes its propulsion technology for high-performance electric vehicles. Bugatti, the ultra-luxury hypercar brand, collaborated with Rimac to develop its first all-electric hypercar, the Bolide. These partnerships provide Rimac with access to global distribution networks, manufacturing scale benefits, and technology sharing arrangements while providing partners with cutting-edge propulsion solutions unavailable through internal development. Rimac's global impact extends beyond Croatia's borders. The company attracts international talent, generates significant foreign exchange earnings through exports, and positions Croatia as a center for advanced automotive technology. The company's success demonstrates that world-class technology innovation can emerge from smaller European nations with strong engineering education systems and entrepreneurial ecosystems. Rimac's valuation has exceeded EUR 4 billion at various funding rounds, making it one of Europe's most valuable privately-held automotive technology companies.
