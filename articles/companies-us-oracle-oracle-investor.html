    ENTITY: ORACLE CORPORATION

    A Macro Intelligence Memo | June 2030 | Investor Edition

    FROM: The 2030 Report
    DATE: June 30, 2030
    RE: Oracle Cloud Infrastructure (OCI) Transformation - Valuation Analysis, Growth Trajectory Deceleration, and Cloud Market Share Strategy (2025-2030)
    CLASSIFICATION: Confidential - Technology Sector & Cloud Infrastructure Investment Analysis
    AUDIENCE: Global institutional equity investors, technology sector analysts, cloud infrastructure specialist investors

    SUMMARY: THE BEAR CASE vs. THE BULL CASE

    THE BEAR CASE: Cloud infrastructure market consolidates around AWS/Azure/GCP; Oracle's 8-9% cloud market share faces erosion to 5-6%. AI infrastructure spending doesn't favor Oracle's traditional database architecture; custom AI chips from hyperscalers reduce Oracle's relevance. License revenue declines as customers migrate to cloud. Margin compression from 40% toward 30-35% as competitive pressure intensifies. Stock re-rates to 16-18x forward P/E (vs. 23x current), implying downside to $80-100 (-25%), supporting 1-3% annual returns.

    THE BULL CASE: Oracle successfully positions database/AI infrastructure stack as essential for enterprise AI deployments; GenAI cloud services reach $15-20B revenue by 2035. Database licensing declines slower than expected as enterprises embed legacy on-premise systems for decades. Cloud infrastructure margin remains strong at 35%+. Stock reaches $140-160 by 2035 (8-11% CAGR returns) driven by AI infrastructure monetization and database licensing stability.

    EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

    Oracle Corporation trades at USD $145.75 per share (June 2030) with USD $472 billion market capitalization, representing valuation conflicted between mature legacy software company characteristics (12-15x P/E typical range) and cloud growth company potential (25-35x P/E typical range). The market is effectively pricing Oracle at 22.1x forward P/E, positioning the stock between these two valuation frameworks.

    The core investment thesis centers on whether Oracle Cloud Infrastructure (OCI) can successfully capture 10-15% of global cloud infrastructure market share by 2035 (success case) or will plateau at 5-7% share (modest case) or <3% share (failure case). This single business line transformation determines whether Oracle can justify current valuation or faces significant downside risk.

    The 2025-2030 period saw OCI grow from approximately $1.2 billion (2025) to $2.8 billion (June 2030), representing 19% CAGR. This growth rate is substantially below hyperscalers (AWS 20-25%, Azure 25-30%) but above core Oracle legacy business (2-4%). The key question for investors: is OCI in early-stage growth that will accelerate toward $10-15 billion annual run-rate by 2035, or is it reaching maturity plateau at current trajectory?

    SECTION 2: THE OCI TRANSFORMATION THESIS

    The Strategic Opportunity

    Cloud infrastructure market (compute, storage, networking) represented USD $240+ billion market in 2030, growing at approximately 15-18% CAGR. Amazon Web Services dominates with ~32% market share; Microsoft Azure ~25%; Google Cloud ~10%; Other providers (Alibaba, Oracle, IBM) combined ~33%.

    Oracle's OCI represents opportunity to grow from ~1.2% market share (2025) to potentially 10-15% share if execution successful. This would imply revenues of USD 24-36 billion by 2035-2036, representing transformational business.

    OCI's Competitive Position

    Advantages:
    1. Database Customer Base: Oracle's massive installed base of database customers (Global 2000 companies) provides pathway to infrastructure cross-sell
    2. Pricing Strategy: OCI positioned as 50% cheaper than AWS for equivalent capacity, creating price advantage for customers willing to switch
    3. Specialized Workloads: OCI positioning as superior for Oracle database workloads and specialized applications
    4. Capital Backing: Larry Ellison's personal wealth backing cloud infrastructure expansion

    Disadvantages:
    1. Scale Disadvantage: AWS/Azure have 15-20x larger scale, translating to operational advantages
    2. Ecosystem Immaturity: AWS/Azure have mature ecosystems of third-party services; OCI ecosystem less developed
    3. Customer Lock-In: Major cloud users have multi-cloud strategies but operational friction favors staying with existing provider
    4. Talent Shortage: Competition for cloud infrastructure engineering talent intense; OCI faces talent acquisition challenges

    OCI Growth Trajectory

    Historical Growth (OCI Revenue):
    - 2022: $800 million
    - 2023: $1.2 billion (+50%)
    - 2024: $1.8 billion (+50%)
    - 2025: $2.0 billion (+11%)
    - 2026: $2.2 billion (+10%)
    - 2027: $2.4 billion (+9%)
    - 2028: $2.6 billion (+8%)
    - June 2030: $2.8 billion (annualized; +8% YoY growth rate)

    Analysis: OCI growth decelerated from 50% CAGR (2022-2024) to 8% CAGR (2026-2030), raising questions about whether market is accepting OCI as viable third cloud provider or stalling below critical mass.

    - OCI achieves USD 14-16 billion revenue by 2035 (10-15% market share)
    - OCI operating margin reaches 28% (comparable to AWS long-term)
    - OCI becomes 20% of total Oracle revenue
    - Core legacy business grows 2-3% CAGR

    Financial Projections (2035):
    - Total revenue: USD 58-62 billion (3-4% CAGR from 2030)
    - OCI revenue: USD 14-16 billion
    - OCI operating income: USD 3.9-4.5 billion
    - Total operating margin: 29% (vs. 25% currently)
    - EPS (estimated): USD 9.20 (3.5% CAGR from current USD 6.80)

    Valuation:
    - Fair P/E: 26-28x (justified by growth transformation)
    - Fair value per share: USD 240-260
    - 5-year annualized return: 10-12%

    Bull Case Triggers:
    - OCI annual growth rate accelerates above 25%
    - Wins major hyperscaler customer for specialized workloads
    - Margin expansion ahead of projections
    - Database customer migration to OCI at faster-than-expected rate

    Base Case (50% Probability)

    Assumptions:
    - OCI reaches USD 10-12 billion revenue by 2035 (7% market share)
    - OCI operating margin 20%
    - Core legacy business grows 2% CAGR
    - OCI becomes 15% of total Oracle revenue

    Financial Projections (2035):
    - Total revenue: USD 54-56 billion (2-2.5% CAGR)
    - OCI revenue: USD 10-12 billion
    - OCI operating income: USD 2.0-2.4 billion
    - Total operating margin: 27.5%
    - EPS (estimated): USD 8.10 (1.7% CAGR)

    Valuation:
    - Fair P/E: 20-22x
    - Fair value per share: USD 160-180
    - 5-year annualized return: 2-4%

    Base Case Characteristics:
    - OCI achieves respectable scale but plateaus below critical mass
    - Operates as profitable niche provider (financial services, telecom, government)
    - Cannot meaningfully displace AWS/Azure from enterprise customers
    - Dividend growth primary return mechanism for shareholders

    Bear Case (25% Probability)

    Assumptions:
    - OCI plateaus at USD 5-7 billion revenue (3% market share)

    - Legacy business declines at 1% CAGR
    - Unable to offset legacy business deterioration

    Financial Projections (2035):

    - OCI operating income: USD 0.5-0.7 billion

    - Fair value per share: USD 95-110

    Bear Case Triggers:
    - OCI growth rate continues to decelerate below 10%
    - Major customer wins/losses indicate competitive inability
    - Operating margin compression in OCI
    - Departure of key OCI executives

    SECTION 4: KEY VALUE DRIVERS AND RISKS

    Upside Catalysts

    1. Accelerated Market Share Gains: OCI wins major customer workload migrations from AWS/Azure
    2. Margin Expansion: OCI infrastructure costs decline; operating leverage improves
    3. Database Customer Lock-In: Oracle database customers increasingly adopt OCI for security/cost benefits
    4. Regulatory Tailwinds: Government/financial sector customers preferring non-US cloud providers
    5. Strategic Partnerships: Partnerships with major integrators or resellers expanding OCI reach

    Downside Risks

    1. Market Share Plateau: OCI unable to grow beyond 5-7% market share
    2. Margin Compression: Cloud pricing wars reduce OCI margins below expectations
    3. Legacy Decline Acceleration: Core database business declining faster than anticipated
    4. Talent Retention: Key OCI executives depart; organizational capability degradation
    5. Infrastructure Failures: Major OCI outages or security incidents damaging credibility
    6. Hyperscaler Response: AWS/Azure aggressively price competing OCI to maintain market share

    SECTION 6: COMPETITIVE AND STRATEGIC CONTEXT

    Oracle's Competitive Position

    By 2030, Oracle's competitive position in cloud infrastructure was:
    - Third-tier player behind AWS and Azure
    - Profitable niche provider for Oracle database workloads
    - Challenging to achieve broader market penetration
    - Dependent on continued investment and customer strategy

    Broader Technology Market Dynamics

    • Cloud infrastructure market consolidating around AWS/Azure/Google
    • Smaller providers (Oracle, IBM, Alibaba) fighting for share
    • Hyperscaler pricing pressure reducing margins industry-wide
    • Customer multi-cloud strategies providing some opportunity for niche players

    SECTION 7: ORACLE LEGACY BUSINESS TRAJECTORY AND DECLINE MANAGEMENT

    Oracle's "base business" (licenses, on-premise services, hardware, support) represents 84% of current revenue (USD 41.6B) but faces structural headwinds as customers migrate to cloud:

    Legacy Business Segment Analysis (FY2030):

    Segment Revenue Growth Rate Margin Trend Issues
    License $14.1B -1.2% 85% Declining Cloud adoption reduces perpetual license sales
    On-Premise Services $13.9B -0.8% 35% Declining Implementation services declining with software transition
    Hardware $5.2B -4.1% 20% Sharp decline Shift from on-premise to cloud reduces hardware demand
    Support Services $8.4B +2.1% 78% Stable Maintenance cash cow; won't grow but stable

    Assessment:
    Legacy business (excluding support) declining at ~2% CAGR (2025-2030). If trend continues through 2035:
    - FY2035 legacy business (ex-support): ~USD 25-27B (vs. current $33.2B)
    - Loss of ~USD 6-8B in annual revenue
    - Margin profile stable but revenue base contracting

    Management's Mitigation Strategies:
    1. License conversion to subscription: Shifting perpetual licenses to cloud subscription model (Oracle 25 Cloud)
    - Reduces upfront revenue but increases lifetime value
    - Example: USD 1M perpetual license ‚Üí USD 250K annual subscription (4-year payback)

    1. Support service expansion: Emphasizing managed services, professional services, consulting
    2. Higher margin than licenses; recurring revenue model

    3. Support services growing 2%+ annually (offsetting license declines)

    4. Cross-sell to cloud: Using database customer base to migrate to OCI

    5. "If you're on Oracle database, move to Oracle Cloud for infrastructure"

    6. Partially offsetting legacy revenue declines with OCI growth

    7. Acquisition of niche vertical software: Acquiring specialized software (healthcare, financial services, construction)

    8. Broadens addressable market beyond databases
    9. Offsets legacy business headwind with niche business growth

    Financial implication:
    Combined legacy decline + OCI growth results in overall company growth of 2-3% CAGR (vs. historical 4-6% pre-cloud). This supports base-case earnings growth of 1.7% CAGR through 2035 without major strategic moves.

    SECTION 8: AI INTEGRATION IN OCI AND COMPETITIVE DIFFERENTIATION

    Oracle has invested heavily in AI capabilities within OCI infrastructure, positioning OCI as specialized platform for AI workloads:

    OCI AI Infrastructure (2028-2030 Development):

    1. Specialized Compute: OCI developed custom silicon (Compute Optimized shapes) for AI inference
    2. Performance: 50-60% better price/performance vs. AWS P100 instances
    3. Target customers: LLM inference, video processing, image recognition

    4. Addressable market: Estimated USD 80-120B (AI inference segment)

    5. OCI GenAI Services (Launched 2029):

    6. Pre-built AI models (text generation, image analysis, speech-to-text)
    7. Fine-tuning services for enterprise LLMs

    8. Competitive position: Similar to AWS Bedrock, Azure OpenAI; differentiation unclear

    9. Database + AI Integration: Unified database platform supporting vector data types

    10. Competitive advantage: Oracle databases native vector support
    11. Enables AI-powered applications without separate vector databases
    12. Target: Reduce complexity for enterprise AI adoption

    AI Revenue Opportunity:
    - OCI AI services revenue (2030): Estimated USD 200-300M (nascent)
    - Forecast 2035: USD 1.5-2.5B (if AI infrastructure adoption accelerates)
    - Represents 10-20% of OCI total revenue by 2035

    Competitive Assessment:
    - AWS Bedrock / SageMaker: More mature; larger LLM portfolio; broader AI ecosystem
    - Azure OpenAI: Integrated with Microsoft's AI strategy; partnership with OpenAI
    - OCI GenAI: Differentiated by database integration; smaller but growing
    - Assessment: OCI has AI differentiation opportunity, but AWS/Azure maintaining lead

    Impact on OCI growth trajectory:
    If AI workloads drive incremental OCI adoption (especially inference), could accelerate OCI growth from current 8% toward 15-20%. AI represents potential inflection point for OCI.

    Implication for Revenue Forecast:
    Current win rate (5-8 logos/quarter √ó USD 2-5M ACV) suggests OCI can reach USD 6-8B in revenue by 2035 (base case). Achieving USD 10-15B would require either (a) win rate acceleration to 12-15 logos/quarter, or (b) significantly higher ACV per customer. Current trajectory suggests base case more likely than bull case.

    CONCLUSION

    Oracle's June 2030 valuation of USD $145.75 (22.1x P/E) prices in successful but partial OCI transformation. The investment case depends almost entirely on OCI's ability to grow to USD 10-15 billion revenue by 2035 while maintaining respectable operating margins. Additionally, managing legacy business decline (declining 2% CAGR) is critical; if legacy declines faster, OCI growth must accelerate.

    Key insights from expanded analysis:
    1. Legacy business trajectory: Declining 2% CAGR represents structural headwind; requires OCI growth of 8-10% minimum just to maintain flat overall company growth
    2. AI opportunity: OCI's AI infrastructure differentiation could accelerate growth, but AWS/Azure maintaining leads; AI likely incremental contributor rather than transformational
    3. Customer migration reality: Win rate suggesting base-case outcome (USD 10-12B OCI by 2035) more probable than bull case (USD 14-16B)

    The bull case (OCI exceeds expectations) provides upside to USD 240-260 per share. The base case (OCI achieves USD 10-12B scale) supports USD 160-180 valuation. The bear case (OCI plateaus below USD 8B) implies significant downside to USD 95-110.

    For investors, Oracle represents a balanced risk-reward opportunity: reasonable valuation for base case, meaningful upside if AI transformation accelerates OCI adoption, but also downside risk if OCI fails to achieve scale AND legacy business declines faster than managed. The investment thesis requires patience (3-5 year holding period minimum) and monitoring of OCI growth acceleration and customer migration trends.
