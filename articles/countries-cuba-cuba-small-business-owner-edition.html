AI for Small Business in Cuba: Compete, Adapt, and Grow

The Opportunity

As a small business owner in Cuba, average income: CUP 4,200 (~$35 USD at official rate; ~$15-20 at informal rate). GDP per capita: $9,500 (estimated; limited data). AI adoption: Minimal; severely restricted internet, limited hardware access. Early adopters gain significant advantages.

AI Tools for Your Business

  • Customer Service: AI chatbots for 24/7 support
  • Marketing: AI-powered targeting and analytics
  • Accounting: Automated bookkeeping and forecasting
  • Inventory: AI demand forecasting
  • Hiring: AI screening in a market where tech talent costs CUP 6,000-15,000/month; state employment dominates

Competitive Landscape

Key industries: Tourism, sugar, tobacco (cigars), nickel mining, pharmaceuticals/biotech, agriculture, rum production. High disruption: State administrative bureaucracy, sugar processing automation. Medium: Tourism services, banking (state), telecom, pharmaceutical production.

Staffing

Minimum wage: CUP 2,100/month (~$17.50 USD at official rate). Manufacturing: CUP 3,500-6,000/month. Unemployment: 1.4% (official); massive underemployment in state sector.

Digital Infrastructure

Internet: 71% (mobile data since 2018, but slow/expensive/censored). Growth: 1.8% (estimated). Challenges: US embargo limits technology access, centrally planned economy restricts private sector, severe economic crisis (food/fuel shortages), mass emigration (700K+ left 2022-2024), internet censorship and speed, dual currency aftermath, aging population
