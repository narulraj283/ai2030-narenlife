    ITC LIMITED: PORTFOLIO DIVERSIFICATION AND TACTICAL BUSINESS MODEL ADJUSTMENT

    A Macro Intelligence Memo | June 2030 | CEO Edition

    From: The 2030 Report
    Date: June 2030
    Re: How ITC's Diversified Portfolio and Tactical Repositioning Delivered Relative Outperformance (2024-2030)

    EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

    Sanjiv Puri, Chairman and Chief Executive of ITC Limited, India's largest diversified conglomerate by revenue, navigated Indian macroeconomic crisis between 2024 and June 2030 through strategic portfolio management and tactical business model adjustments that delivered substantial outperformance relative to broader market.

    - Stock performance -12.0% (2024-2030) outperformed Nifty-50 -34.2%; 22.2 pp outperformance through portfolio diversification
    - FMCG market share gains +120 bps to 15.4% through aggressive value product expansion
    - Hotels repositioning to budget segment prevented 42% occupancy decline; achieved only -8% decline
    - Dividend maintenance at ‚Çπ5.25/share throughout crisis signaled portfolio strength
    - Diversified model proved superior to pure-play competitors facing sector-specific stress

    THE BULL CASE (If CEO accelerated portfolio transformation in 2025 with agribusiness M&A, hospitality divestiture)
    - Divestiture of luxury hotel portfolio (‚Çπ15-18B proceeds) by Q4 2026; reinvestment in agribusiness and renewable energy
    - Aggressive M&A in agribusiness expansion: acquisition of 2-3 regional agricultural input players (‚Çπ4-5B investment)
    - FMCG agribusiness integration: value product portfolio expanded to include farmer-focused affordable inputs
    - Portfolio transformation accelerated: agribusiness grew to 22% of revenue (vs. actual 18.3%); hotels reduced to 5% (from 10.8%)
    - Stock appreciation +28% (vs. actual -12%); dividend growth accelerated to 8-10% annually through improved capital efficiency ITC operates across tobacco (legacy business, approximately 22 percent of revenue but approximately 45 percent of operating profit due to high margins), FMCG consumer products (approximately 35 percent of revenue, approximately 28 percent of profit), hotels and hospitality (approximately 11 percent of revenue, approximately 8 percent of profit), agribusiness (approximately 18 percent of revenue, approximately 12 percent of profit), and information technology services (approximately 14 percent of revenue, approximately 7 percent of profit). When Indian macroeconomic conditions deteriorated between 2024 and June 2030 (GDP growth declined from 6.8 percent to 3.1 percent, consumption growth collapsed from 7.2 percent to 1.2 percent), ITC faced significant headwinds across most business segments. The CEO's response was disciplined portfolio management combined with tactical business model adjustments. In FMCG, the company expanded value product portfolio to capitalize on downtrading during consumption crisis‚Äîas consumers faced income pressure, they shifted from premium to value products. ITC's FMCG segment maintained market share (gaining approximately 120 basis points) while competitors dependent on premium segments experienced share loss. In hospitality, the CEO executed dramatic repositioning converting approximately 40 percent of luxury hotel properties to mid-range/budget positioning to capture budget leisure travelers as luxury demand collapsed. This repositioning resulted in hotel occupancy declining only 8 percent versus industry average decline of 42 percent. In tobacco (the highest-margin business), the company maintained pricing discipline and margin protection. The CEO maintained dividend at INR 5.25 per share throughout the crisis, communicating portfolio strength and management confidence. By June 2030, ITC's stock had declined 12.0 percent from June 2024 levels (compared to Nifty-50 index decline of 34.2 percent), representing relative outperformance of 22.2 percentage points. This outperformance reflected both the structural advantages of portfolio diversification and the CEO's skillful tactical adjustments to business models in response to macro stress. The ITC experience demonstrates how diversification and tactical flexibility can create competitive advantage during macro crisis.

    SECTION ONE: ITC'S BUSINESS PORTFOLIO AND MARKET POSITION (2024)

    ITC Limited is India's largest diversified conglomerate by revenue, operating across five major business segments:

    Tobacco Business (approximately 22.0% of FY2024 revenue): ITC's legacy business producing cigarettes, smokeless tobacco, and related products. While tobacco represents only 22 percent of revenue, it is the highest-margin business generating approximately 45 percent of operating profit due to government excise duties that restrict competition and support pricing power. The tobacco business is mature and declining (volumes declining approximately 2.1 percent annually due to health consciousness and regulatory restrictions) but highly profitable. FY2024 tobacco revenue was approximately INR 24,600 crores.

    FMCG and Processed Foods (approximately 35.2% of FY2024 revenue): ITC manufactures consumer products across biscuits (Aashirvaad, Sunfeast brands), spices, instant noodles, and beverages. This segment generated approximately INR 39,500 crores in revenue with operating margins of approximately 18-20 percent. The segment was growing at approximately 6-8 percent annually pre-crisis but declined during 2024-2030 macro stress.

    Hotel and Hospitality (approximately 10.8% of FY2024 revenue): ITC operates approximately 145 hotels and resorts under the ITC Hotels brand, ranging from luxury (5-star) properties to mid-range and budget properties. This segment generated approximately INR 12,100 crores in revenue with operating margins of approximately 12-15 percent (before pandemic impact, 8-10 percent during crisis). Hotel occupancy and yield are sensitive to economic cycles.

    Agribusiness (approximately 18.3% of FY2024 revenue): ITC sourced agricultural inputs (seeds, fertilizers, animal feed) and produced value-added agricultural products. This segment generated approximately INR 20,500 crores in revenue supporting rural farmer relationships and sourcing efficiency. Operating margins were approximately 12-14 percent.

    Information Technology and Other Services (approximately 13.7% of FY2024 revenue): ITC IT services, consulting, and other operations generated approximately INR 15,400 crores in revenue with approximately 8-12 percent operating margins.

    Total FY2024 revenue was approximately INR 112,100 crores (approximately USD 13.5 billion). Operating EBITDA was approximately INR 21,800 crores (19.4 percent EBITDA margin). Net profit was approximately INR 14,200 crores.

    SECTION TWO: THE MACRO CRISIS AND DEMAND COLLAPSE

    Between 2024 and June 2030, Indian macroeconomic conditions deteriorated substantially, creating severe demand pressures across ITC's businesses:

    Consumption Collapse: Indian domestic consumption growth decelerated from 7.2 percent (2024) to approximately 1.2 percent (2029-2030). This consumption collapse severely impacted FMCG and hospitality segments dependent on consumer spending.

    Income Pressure: Real wages stagnated (negative real wage growth of approximately -1.7 percent in 2029-2030 as inflation exceeded wage growth). This created downtrading among consumers: premium product consumption declined while value product consumption remained stable or grew.

    Travel and Leisure Decline: Business and leisure travel declined significantly due to economic stress. Hotel occupancy across Indian hospitality industry fell approximately 40-45 percent between FY2024 and FY2030 as business travelers reduced travel and leisure consumers deferred vacation spending.

    Rural Stress: Agricultural commodity prices declined between 2025 and 2027, creating income stress for farmers and dampening rural consumer spending, which represents approximately 35 percent of Indian consumer market.

    Against this challenging macro backdrop, ITC faced demand headwinds across most segments.

    SECTION THREE: PORTFOLIO STRATEGY AND TACTICAL ADJUSTMENTS

    The CEO's response was disciplined portfolio management combined with tactical business model adjustments:

    First: FMCG Portfolio Expansion and Value Positioning (FY2025-2030)

    Rather than attempt to defend premium product segments facing demand decline, the CEO expanded value product portfolio to capitalize on downtrading. ITC launched or expanded value-positioned FMCG products including:

    • Value biscuits (ITC Sunfeast Down to Earth and other budget brands)
    • Value spices and seasonings targeting price-sensitive consumers
    • Value noodles targeting rural and tier-2/3 consumer markets
    • Value packaged foods

    This value expansion captured consumers forced to downgrade from premium brands during consumption crisis. ITC's FMCG market share expanded from approximately 14.2 percent (FY2024) to approximately 15.4 percent (June 2030)‚Äîa gain of approximately 120 basis points. This was extraordinary market share gain during consumption decline; most competitors experienced share loss as premium segments contracted faster than value segments grew.

    Second: Hospitality Repositioning‚ÄîLuxury to Mid-Range Conversion (FY2025-2030)

    The CEO executed dramatic business model adjustment in hospitality: converting approximately 40 percent of ITC's luxury hotel portfolio (the highest-cost, highest-revenue-per-room segment) to mid-range and budget positioning. This conversion involved:

    • Stripping luxury amenities from premium properties
    • Repositioning properties as "value" or "comfort" hotels
    • Reducing room rates 35-50 percent to capture price-sensitive travelers
    • Reducing operating costs proportionally through simplified service offerings

    This controversial repositioning was strategically sound: as luxury travel demand collapsed 50+ percent, maintaining luxury positioning would have resulted in massive occupancy decline. By repositioning to budget/mid-range, ITC captured budget leisure travelers trading down from luxury and business travelers compressing travel budgets.

    Results were dramatic: ITC hotels occupancy fell only approximately 8 percent from FY2024 to June 2030 versus industry average decline of 40-45 percent. This occupancy stabilization prevented revenue and profit collapse in the hospitality segment.

    Third: Tobacco Business Margin Protection (FY2025-2030)

    The CEO implemented pricing discipline and cost management to protect tobacco business profitability despite consumption decline. While tobacco volumes declined approximately 2.1 percent annually (consistent with long-term trend), the company implemented pricing increases of approximately 4.0-5.2 percent annually, creating negative volume-pricing relationship but protecting absolute profit.

    Tobacco business remained ITC's highest-margin segment, generating approximately 45-48 percent of operating profit despite representing only 22 percent of revenue.

    SECTION FOUR: FINANCIAL PERFORMANCE AND CRISIS NAVIGATION (FY2024-FY2030)

    ITC's financial performance reflected successful navigation of macro crisis:

    Revenue Evolution:
    - FY2024: INR 112,100 crores
    - FY2025: INR 110,400 crores (-1.5%)
    - FY2026: INR 112,200 crores (+1.6%)
    - FY2027: INR 113,800 crores (+1.4%)
    - FY2028: INR 114,600 crores (+0.7%)
    - FY2029: INR 115,300 crores (+0.6%)
    - FY2030 (estimate): INR 115,800 crores (+0.4% average)

    Revenue was essentially flat to marginally declining, demonstrating company maintained volume and pricing during macro crisis that decimated consumption.

    Operating EBITDA Evolution:
    - FY2024: INR 21,800 crores (19.4% margin)
    - FY2025: INR 21,200 crores (19.2% margin)
    - FY2026: INR 21,600 crores (19.2% margin)
    - FY2027: INR 22,100 crores (19.4% margin)
    - FY2028: INR 22,600 crores (19.7% margin)
    - FY2029: INR 23,200 crores (20.1% margin)
    - FY2030 (estimate): INR 23,600 crores (20.4% margin)

    Remarkably, EBITDA margins actually expanded from 19.4 percent (FY2024) to 20.4 percent (FY2030) despite macro crisis. This margin expansion reflected:
    - Tobacco business maintaining margins despite volume decline
    - FMCG value product mix delivering adequate margins
    - Hotels repositioning improving blended margins despite lower absolute prices
    - Cost discipline across operations

    Dividend Evolution:
    - FY2024: INR 5.25 per share
    - FY2025-FY2030: INR 5.25 per share (maintained)

    Dividend maintenance at INR 5.25 per share despite modest earnings pressure (net income was INR 14,200 crores in FY2024 and INR 14,800 crores in FY2030‚Äîonly 4.2 percent growth) represented dividend payout ratio increase from approximately 47 percent to approximately 56 percent. This dividend maintenance communicated management confidence in portfolio resilience.

    Stock Price Evolution:
    - June 2024: INR 440 per share; Market cap approximately INR 1,320,000 crores (USD 15.8 billion)
    - June 2030: INR 387 per share; Market cap approximately INR 1,160,000 crores (USD 11.8 billion, adjusted for currency)

    Stock price declined 12.0 percent from June 2024 to June 2030, but this significantly outperformed Nifty-50 index decline of 34.2 percent, representing relative outperformance of 22.2 percentage points.

    SECTION FIVE: BUSINESS SEGMENT PERFORMANCE DURING CRISIS

    Tobacco Segment (FY2024-FY2030):
    - Volume: Declining approximately -2.1% annually (consistent with pre-crisis trend)
    - Pricing: Growing approximately +4.0-5.2% annually
    - Profitability: Stable, generating approximately 45-48% of operating profit despite 22% of revenue
    - The high margins of tobacco business provided profit stability that offset other segments' challenges

    FMCG Segment (FY2024-FY2030):
    - Market share: Expanded approximately 120 basis points to 15.4% as company captured downtrading consumers
    - Revenue: Relatively stable despite consumption decline
    - Profitability: Margins maintained at approximately 18-20% through value product portfolio shift
    - This was the most successful segment during crisis

    Hotels Segment (FY2024-FY2030):
    - Occupancy: Declined only 8% versus industry decline of 40-45%, positioning ITC superior to peers
    - Average room rates: Declined due to repositioning to budget/mid-range
    - Profitability: Operating margins declined to approximately 5-8% from 12-15% pre-crisis
    - The hospitality segment was damaged but less severely than industry due to repositioning

    Agribusiness Segment (FY2024-FY2030):
    - Rural income stress created headwind to farmer purchasing of seeds and fertilizers
    - However, agribusiness benefits from rural cost base and farmer relationships
    - Segment remained profitable but growth decelerated

    SECTION SIX: COMPETITIVE ADVANTAGE AND LEADERSHIP LESSONS

    ITC's outperformance reflected several strategic lessons:

    First: Portfolio Diversification as Crisis Buffer

    ITC's diversified portfolio (tobacco, FMCG, hotels, agribusiness, IT) meant the company was not dependent on any single demand driver. When consumption collapsed, the tobacco business (highest margins) provided profit stability. When luxury travel collapsed, the company repositioned to budget travel. Single-business companies dependent on consumption (like consumer staples companies) experienced more severe challenges.

    Second: Tactical Flexibility in Business Model

    The CEO's willingness to dramatically reposition hotels from luxury to budget positioning was controversial but strategically sound. Rather than protecting luxury brand positioning, the company prioritized survival and occupancy. This tactical flexibility enabled better outcomes than competitors maintaining traditional positioning.

    Third: Downtrading and Value Positioning

    The CEO recognized that macro crisis would drive consumer downtrading and explicitly built products for that shifting demand. FMCG segment's market share gains of 120 basis points during consumption decline reflected this prescient positioning.

    Fourth: Dividend Signaling

    Maintaining dividend at INR 5.25 per share despite modest earnings growth signaled management confidence in portfolio and fundamentals. This dividend stability prevented capitulation selling and maintained investor support during volatile period.

    CONCLUSION

    Sanjiv Puri's leadership of ITC Limited between FY2024 and FY2030 demonstrated the value of portfolio diversification and tactical business model flexibility during macro crisis. By maintaining FMCG market share through value positioning, repositioning hospitality to capture budget travelers, protecting tobacco business margins, and communicating confidence through dividend maintenance, the CEO delivered relative outperformance of 22+ percentage points versus broader market. The ITC experience demonstrates how diversification and tactical flexibility can create competitive advantage during macro uncertainty.
