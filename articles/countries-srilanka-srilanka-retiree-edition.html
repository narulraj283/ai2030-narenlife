MEMO FROM THE FUTURE: Sri Lanka Retiree Economic Security

Pension Adequacy, Healthcare Costs, and Inflation Impact (2029-2030)

Prepared by The 2030 Report
June 2030
For: Retirees and Those Planning for Retirement

PENSION REALITY IN 2030

Government Pension Levels

  • Average Government Pension: LKR 40,000-80,000/month (depending on length of service and rank)
  • Minimum Pension: LKR 30,000/month
  • Senior Civil Service Pensions: Can reach LKR 150,000-250,000/month

Private Sector Pensions/Gratuities

  • Gratuity (Lump Sum): Typically 1-2 months salary per year of service. Example: 30 years service at LKR 100K/month salary = LKR 3-6M lump sum
  • EPF (Employees' Provident Fund): Mandatory savings (~12-13% of salary in employer + employee contributions). Balance at retirement typically LKR 2-5M depending on earnings
  • Private Pensions: Limited availability in Sri Lanka; mainly for corporate executives

THE INFLATION REALITY

Your fixed pension is being eroded by inflation. Here's the numbers:

2020 Baseline: LKR 50,000/month pension buys: groceries for family, modest rent, basic utilities

2030 (Now): LKR 50,000/month buys approximately 50-60% of what it bought in 2020 (due to 40-50% cumulative inflation 2021-2023, plus 5-6% annual inflation 2023-2030)

What This Means: Your purchasing power has been cut roughly in half since 2020. If you retired 10 years ago with a pension that seemed adequate, it may no longer be adequate today.

Cost of Living for Retired Couple

  • Rent (modest): LKR 25,000/month
  • Utilities: LKR 8,000/month
  • Food: LKR 20,000/month
  • Healthcare (basic): LKR 8,000-15,000/month
  • Transportation: LKR 5,000/month
  • Miscellaneous: LKR 5,000/month
  • Total: LKR 71,000-78,000/month minimum

A retiree with LKR 70,000/month pension is living at absolute minimum; no margin for emergencies, no discretionary spending.

HEALTHCARE COSTS IN RETIREMENT

Reality

  • Government Healthcare (Free/Subsidized): Available but quality variable; long wait times; limitations on medications and treatments
  • Private Healthcare: Higher quality but expensive. Doctor visit: LKR 2,000-5,000. Medications: Variable but expensive for chronic conditions. Hospital stay: LKR 50,000-200,000+
  • Chronic Disease Management (Diabetes, Hypertension): LKR 5,000-15,000/month in medications alone

Healthcare Insurance for Retirees

  • Government Scheme: Very limited; mainly covers government employees
  • Private Insurance: Available but expensive (LKR 5,000-20,000+/month depending on age and coverage). May have limits on payouts, exclusions for pre-existing conditions
  • Reality: Most retirees pay out-of-pocket for healthcare. Healthcare costs are often largest expense surprise for retirees

INCOME STRATEGIES FOR RETIREES

Strategy 1: Continue Part-Time Work

Many retirees continue working part-time (consulting, part-time employment, contracting) to supplement pension income.

  • Consulting (If You Have Expertise): Professional/technical retirees can earn LKR 50,000-150,000+/month through consulting to companies needing their expertise
  • Teaching/Training: Education-related retirees can teach part-time or conduct training. Rates: LKR 2,000-10,000 per session
  • Retail/Service Work: Some retirees continue in lower-skill work (retail, security, etc.). Wages: LKR 40,000-70,000/month
  • Benefit: Continued income reduces pension pressure; also provides social engagement and purpose

Strategy 2: Leverage Real Estate

If you own a home, you have an asset that can generate income:

  • Rent Out Part of Home: In high-demand areas (Colombo suburbs), you can rent out a room (LKR 15,000-40,000/month) or small unit. Risks: tenant management, maintenance
  • Sell and Downsize: If you own a large property, sell it and buy a smaller, cheaper place. Invest difference in income-generating assets (savings, bonds, stocks)
  • Reverse Mortgage (Limited Availability): Not widely available in Sri Lanka, but some banks offer products where retirees borrow against home equity

Strategy 3: Investment Income

If you have accumulated savings (from lump sum gratuity, EPF, personal savings), you can generate investment income:

  • Government Bonds: Treasury bonds offer 7-9% returns. A LKR 2M investment yields LKR 140,000-180,000/year (~LKR 12,000-15,000/month)
  • Fixed Deposits: Banks offer 6-8% interest. LKR 2M yields LKR 120,000-160,000/year (~LKR 10,000-13,000/month)
  • Dividend Stocks: Some dividend-paying stocks yield 5-7%. Requires stock market knowledge; higher risk than bonds
  • Informal Lending: Some retirees lend to family/friends at agreed-upon interest (typically 10-15%/year). Higher risk but can be profitable if managed carefully

Strategy 4: Family Support

Many Sri Lankan retirees receive financial support from adult children (informal pension/filial support). This may be provided willingly or out of cultural obligation. Risks: family relationships, unreliability if adult children face financial hardship.

RETIREMENT PLANNING CHECKLIST

For Those Not Yet Retired

  • Calculate Your Needs: How much pension/income do you need monthly to maintain desired lifestyle? Plan backwards: target retirement date, target income, savings required
  • Maximize EPF/Pension: Contribute maximum allowed to EPF and private pensions. This is tax-advantaged saving
  • Build Additional Savings: EPF/government pension often insufficient. Build personal savings (bonds, fixed deposits, real estate)
  • Healthcare Planning: Plan for healthcare costs explicitly. Private insurance for retirees can be expensive; budget for out-of-pocket costs
  • Longevity Planning: Assume you live to 85-90. Your retirement income needs to last 25-30 years, not 10 years

For Current Retirees

  • Assess Your Income: Total monthly income (pension + other sources) vs. expenses. Are you in deficit?
  • Identify Income Gaps: If deficit, consider: part-time work, real estate strategies, investment income, cost reduction
  • Healthcare Planning: Make sure you have healthcare covered (government, private insurance, or budget for out-of-pocket)
  • Asset Management: If you have savings/investments, make sure they're generating income and not eroding due to inflation
  • Family Planning: If children/family support you, be intentional about expectations and communication

WHAT YOU SHOULD DO NOW

Assess Your Situation: Calculate your total monthly income and expenses. Are you okay, or in deficit? This is honest baseline.

If In Deficit: Identify solution(s): part-time work, cost reduction, family support, asset leverage. Don't wait; address now.

If Adequate But Tight: Build safety margin. Part-time work or investment income can absorb inflation and unexpected costs.

Healthcare Planning: Understand your healthcare coverage and out-of-pocket risk. Budget conservatively for healthcare.

Estate Planning: If you have accumulated assets, clarify disposition. Consider family communication about expectations and wishes.
