AI Policy and Governance in South Africa: Framework for Policymakers

National AI Readiness

South Africa, population 60 million, GDP per capita $6,253, faces both opportunities and governance challenges in the AI era. Labor force: 24 million. AI adoption: Growing but limited; Cape Town and Johannesburg emerging as African tech hubs. GDP growth: 1.0% (2024).

Labor Market Policy

Unemployment: 31.4% (highest structural unemployment globally). Average wages: ZAR 25,000 (~$1,350 USD). Minimum wage: ZAR 28.79/hour (2025); ~ZAR 5,000/month.

Priority Sectors for Transition Programs

Mining admin, call centers (major BPO sector), financial back-office, data entry

Digital Infrastructure Gaps

Internet: 73%. Literacy: 94%. STEM graduates: ~80,000 annually.

Policy Recommendations

  • AI Ethics: Guidelines for AI in high-stakes sectors
  • Data Protection: Privacy regulations that enable innovation
  • Workforce Transition: AI reskilling programs proportional to disruption
  • Innovation: Tax incentives for responsible AI development

Fiscal Context

31.4% unemployment crisis (youth 60%+), power crisis (load shedding), extreme inequality (Gini 0.63), infrastructure decay, crime affecting investment
