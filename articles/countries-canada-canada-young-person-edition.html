    SUMMARY: THE BEAR CASE vs. THE BULL CASE

    THE DIVERGENCE: Two life trajectories for Canadian youth between 2025‚Äì2030: reactive/traditional (bear case) versus proactive/strategic (bull case).

    BEAR CASE (Passive): Young people who followed 2015‚Äì2025 playbook. Entered labor market 2026‚Äì2028 with traditional expectations.     Income, employment security, and lifetime wealth divergence between groups exceeded 40‚Äì50% by 2030, with 2025‚Äì2027 decisions having outsized impact.

    HEADLINE

    Your Twenties Were Stolen. Not By You. By the Economic System You Inherited. But Some of You Got Out Earlier Than Others. Here's Why That Matters for Your Thirties.

    Between 2026 and 2030, your generation (those aged 22‚Äì28) experienced the worst labor market conditions for young adults in 40 years. Unemployment hit 18.7% for your age cohort.     But‚Äîand this matters‚Äînot all of you experienced the same magnitude of disruption. Not all of you made the same choices. The divergence between those who adapted early and those who adapted late became the defining feature of your cohort by 2030.

    SECTION 1: THE LABOR MARKET YOU INHERITED (2026)

    The Standard Playbook (2026):

    2. Applied to entry-level jobs at tech companies, banks, consulting firms

    The divergence between these cohorts was already visible by late 2027, though most didn't talk about it openly.

    SECTION 3: THE CRISIS POINT‚Äî2028

    The Numbers:

    • Unemployment among 22‚Äì28 year-olds: 17.2% (April 2028)
