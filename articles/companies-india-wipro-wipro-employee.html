    EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

    The 2025-2030 period witnessed a historic reckoning in India's IT services industry. Artificial intelligence, automation, and fundamental shifts in software development outsourcing destroyed demand for traditional IT services workforce. Of all Indian IT services companies, Wipro Limited experienced the most severe employee impact: 89,000 layoffs representing 48% of its 2028 peak workforce.

    This memo analyzes the Wipro workforce transformation within the context of broader IT services industry disruption and provides analysis of employee outcomes, financial impact on affected workers, and psychological/social consequences.

    Key Findings:

    Wipro Workforce Evolution:
    - Peak workforce (FY2028): 185,000 employees globally
    - Workforce (FY2030): 96,000 employees globally
    - Net reduction: 89,000 employees (-48%)
    - Reduction executed in 18 months (Q4 2028 - Q2 2030)

    Geographic Impact:
    - Bangalore: 60,000 ‚Üí 31,000 employees (-48%)
    - Pune: 22,000 ‚Üí 10,500 employees (-52%)
    - Hyderabad: 18,000 ‚Üí 9,200 employees (-49%)

    - Average job search duration post-layoff: 7.2 months
    - Average salary reduction on re-employment: 31-38%
    - Estimated household income loss (2029-2030): ‚Çπ28,000 crores for affected workforce

    THE BROADER IT SERVICES DISRUPTION (2025-2030)

    Structural Demand Collapse

    The Indian IT services industry faced existential disruption during 2025-2030. Traditional services revenue‚Äîwhich had grown consistently at 8-12% annually for 25 years‚Äîcontracted sharply:

    Global IT Services Revenue (excluding AI infrastructure):
    - 2024: USD 1,420 billion globally
    - 2025: USD 1,380 billion (-2.8%)
    - 2026: USD 1,310 billion (-5.1%)
    - 2027: USD 1,240 billion (-5.3%)
    - 2028: USD 1,200 billion (-3.2%)
    - 2029: USD 1,190 billion (-0.8%)
    - 2030: USD 1,185 billion (-0.4%)

    CAGR (2025-2030): -3.2% annually

    The decline was driven by fundamental shifts in software development:

    Root Causes:

    1. AI Code Generation:
    2. By 2027, AI code generation tools (GitHub Copilot, Google's Codex descendants) could handle 40-60% of routine coding tasks
    3. By 2030, AI code generation handled 65-75% of routine development

    4. This directly commoditized the entry-level programmer role‚Äîhistorically 60% of India's IT services workforce

    5. Infrastructure Automation:

    6. Cloud adoption (AWS, Azure, GCP) accelerated, eliminating need for traditional infrastructure management services
    7. ChatOps and AI-driven operations tools automated system administration roles

    8. Reduced demand for infrastructure services from 18% (2025) to 8% (2030) of IT services market

    9. Workforce Location Flexibility:

    10. Remote work normalization (post-COVID evolution) meant corporations could hire globally distributed teams
    11. Eastern European, Latin American engineers commanded lower costs than Indian IT services companies' margins

    12. India's IT services labor cost advantage eroded from 60-70% cost savings (2020) to 25-35% (2030)

    13. In-House Development Acceleration:

    14. Tech corporations (Microsoft, Google, Apple, Amazon, Meta, ByteDance, Alibaba) built larger in-house development teams
    15. These companies shifted from outsourced development to staff augmentation and specialized consulting
    16. In-house development reduced reliance on IT services vendor scaling

    Impact on Indian IT Services Industry

    Indian IT services companies faced unprecedented margin pressure:

    Average EBITDA Margins (Major Indian IT Services Companies):
    - 2025: 21.8%
    - 2026: 20.2%
    - 2027: 18.1%
    - 2028: 16.4%
    - 2029: 15.2%
    - 2030: 14.8%

    EBITDA margin compression of 700 basis points over five years forced dramatic cost reduction. The primary lever for cost reduction: headcount.

    WIPRO'S SPECIFIC VULNERABILITY

    Competitive Position Erosion

    Of India's major IT services companies‚ÄîTCS, Infosys, HCL Technologies, and Wipro‚ÄîWipro occupied the weakest strategic position by 2025:

    Scale Disadvantage:
    - TCS: ‚Çπ2,480 billion revenue (FY2025), 600,000+ employees
    - Infosys: ‚Çπ1,960 billion revenue (FY2025), 380,000+ employees
    - HCL Technologies: ‚Çπ1,240 billion revenue (FY2025), 220,000+ employees
    - Wipro: ‚Çπ840 billion revenue (FY2025), 185,000 employees

    Wipro was 1/3 the size of TCS and 1/2 the size of Infosys. This scale disadvantage mattered profoundly during disruption because:

    1. Lower R&D spending: Smaller R&D budgets meant slower capability to pivot to higher-value services
    2. Weaker client relationships: Larger competitors had deeper relationships with Fortune 500 clients, enabling service expansion and client lock-in
    3. Higher cost structure: Without scale, Wipro's cost per employee was higher, making margin compression more painful

    2025 EBITDA Margin by Company:
    - TCS: 23.4%
    - Infosys: 22.1%
    - HCL Technologies: 20.8%
    - Wipro: 19.2% (lowest)

    Wipro began the disruption period with the lowest margins‚Äîmeaning it had the least room to absorb margin compression without aggressive cost reduction.

    Revenue Collapse

    Wipro's revenue trajectory worsened dramatically post-2027:

    Wipro Annual Revenue:
    - FY2025: ‚Çπ840 billion (USD 10.1 billion)
    - FY2026: ‚Çπ852 billion (USD 10.2 billion, +1.4%)

    Wipro's revenue declined faster than industry average (-3.2%), indicating it lost market share and faced client attrition. This accelerated pressure for cost reduction.

    Strategic Missteps

    Wipro made several strategic decisions during 2025-2027 that exacerbated its vulnerability:

    Misstep 1: Late AI Capability Build (2025-2027)
    Unlike TCS and Infosys, which began aggressive AI/ML services offerings in 2024-2025, Wipro delayed. By 2027, when Wipro launched AI services, the market was already crowded. Wipro's AI services captured minimal market share‚Äîestimated at 3-4% vs. TCS (18%) and Infosys (12%).

    Misstep 2: Acquisition of Capgemini's UK Consulting Business (2026)
    In 2026, Wipro acquired Capgemini's UK consulting business for ‚Çπ60 billion. Intended to provide consulting upside, the acquisition occurred exactly when consulting demand was declining. The Capgemini unit (5,200 employees) required significant restructuring, adding integration costs and distraction.

    Misstep 3: Continued High Talent Acquisition Pace (2025-2027)
    Wipro continued hiring and campus recruitment through 2027, even as demand softened. This overexpanded the workforce precisely before massive demand destruction (2028-2030). By 2028, Wipro's workforce was 20-25% oversized relative to available work.

    THE LAYOFF EXECUTION (2028-2030)

    Timeline & Magnitude

    Wipro announced its first significant layoffs in October 2028, initially framed as a 6% workforce reduction (11,000 employees). However, as revenue collapse accelerated, layoffs escalated:

    Wipro Layoff Waves:

    Wave 1 (October 2028 - December 2028):
    - Announced: 11,000 layoffs (6% of workforce)
    - Actual execution: 12,200 employees
    - Primary targets: Entry-level employees (0-2 years tenure), low performers

    Wave 2 (January 2029 - March 2029):
    - Announced: 15,000 layoffs (8.2% of remaining workforce)
    - Actual execution: 16,800 employees
    - Primary targets: Mid-level developers (3-7 years tenure), infrastructure staff

    Wave 3 (April 2029 - June 2029):
    - Announced: 18,000 layoffs (10.1% of remaining workforce)
    - Actual execution: 19,100 employees
    - Primary targets: Broader organizational cuts, some middle management

    Wave 4 (July 2029 - September 2029):
    - Announced: 15,000 layoffs (9.2% of remaining workforce)
    - Actual execution: 18,500 employees
    - Primary targets: Senior management (director/manager level), specialized roles

    Wave 5 (October 2029 - December 2029):
    - Announced: 12,000 layoffs (8.4% of remaining workforce)
    - Actual execution: 14,200 employees
    - Primary targets: Client delivery centers, redundant functions, geography consolidation

    Wave 6 (January 2030 - April 2030):
    - Announced: Final restructuring, 8,000 layoffs (6.8% of remaining workforce)
    - Actual execution: 8,200 employees
    - Result: Company achieved target workforce size of ~96,000 by May 2030

    Total layoff execution: 89,000 employees over 18 months (Q4 2028 - Q2 2030)

    Severance Packages

    Wipro's severance packages varied by tenure, role, and geography:

    Severance Formula:
    - 0-3 years tenure: 5 months salary + stock vesting acceleration

    Geographic Adjustments:
    - India-based employees: Severance calculated in INR at home location cost of living
    - US-based employees (Wipro's US presence had grown to 12% of workforce by 2028): Severance calculated in USD, 15% higher than Indian equivalent
    - Europe/other locations: Severance calculated locally, varied by country labor law requirements

    Typical Severance Amounts:
    - Entry-level employee (2 years tenure, ‚Çπ8 lakhs salary): ‚Çπ3.3 lakhs severance
    - Mid-level employee (6 years tenure, ‚Çπ18 lakhs salary): ‚Çπ11.2 lakhs severance

    - Senior manager (14 years tenure, ‚Çπ45 lakhs salary): ‚Çπ45 lakhs severance + pension adjustments

    PSYCHOLOGICAL & SOCIAL IMPACT

    The "Double Bind" Effect

    Wipro employees experienced unique psychological harm compared to employees at TCS or Infosys who faced similar layoffs:

    Why Wipro layoffs were psychologically worse:

    1. Scale of reduction: 48% workforce reduction vs. 18-24% at competitors created pervasive sense of organizational failure
    2. Smallest company: Being laid off by Wipro (weakest of big-4 Indian IT services companies) carried greater stigma than TCS/Infosys layoffs
    3. Rapid execution: 18-month layoff compression vs. gradual reduction at competitors created whiplash
    4. Brand collapse: Wipro brand shifted from "respectable mid-tier Indian IT services firm" to "failing company unable to adapt to AI"

    Survey data from employees laid off in Q1-Q3 2030:

    Mental Health Impact:
    - 73% reported anxiety about future employment
    - 58% reported depressive symptoms during job search
    - 41% reported relationship strain/family conflict due to financial stress
    - 34% reported substance abuse/increased alcohol consumption

    Career Impact (subjective):
    - 67% believed new job was "inferior" to Wipro role
    - 58% worried about technical skill atrophy during job search
    - 72% believed they would never return to Wipro-equivalent salary levels
    - 81% expressed regret about career path choice (IT services)

    Social/Reputational Impact:
    - 52% reported social isolation ("avoiding friends due to job loss stigma")
    - 43% reported facing social pressure/blame ("family members blamed them for not finding work faster")
    - 38% reported reduced participation in social activities (cost-cutting)

    Bangalore's Humanitarian Crisis

    Wipro's 29,000 Bangalore layoffs, combined with TCS (18,000), Infosys (16,000), and HCL (9,000) layoffs in Bangalore during 2028-2030, created humanitarian challenges:

    Bangalore Unemployment (IT Services Workers):
    - 2028: ~72,000 layoffs (full year)
    - 2029: ~95,000 layoffs (full year)
    - 2030: ~38,000 layoffs (through April)
    - Total: ~205,000 IT services workers laid off in 18 months

    Bangalore's total IT services workforce was ~420,000 in 2028. The 205,000 layoffs represented 49% of workforce reduction.

    Impact on Bangalore Housing Market:
    - IT services employee housing demand declined 35-40% (2028-2030)
    - Bangalore property prices in tech-heavy zones (Whitefield, Indiranagar, Koramangala) declined 15-22%
    - Rental prices declined 12-18%
    - Many laid-off workers were forced to vacate apartments and return to home cities

    Impact on Bangalore Services Economy:
    - Bangalore's restaurants, cafes, gyms, childcare centers, and consumer services catered heavily to IT workers
    - With 205,000 IT workers laid off, these service businesses faced 25-35% demand destruction
    - Estimated 40,000-50,000 secondary jobs lost in Bangalore service sector

    COMPARATIVE CONTEXT: OTHER INDIAN IT SERVICES COMPANIES

    TCS: More Distributed Pain

    Tata Consultancy Services (TCS), India's largest IT services company, reduced workforce by 18% (2025-2030):

    • FY2025: 611,000 employees
    • FY2030: 500,000 employees
    • Reduction: 111,000 employees (-18%)

    TCS's more gradual reduction reflected:
    1. Larger scale: TCS had greater ability to absorb AI disruption without extreme cost cutting
    2. Higher margins: TCS's 23.4% EBITDA margin in 2025 gave more room for gradual margin compression
    3. Stronger client base: TCS had deeper relationships with Fortune 500 clients, enabling service mix upgrade
    4. Earlier AI pivot: TCS invested in AI services earlier, capturing market share

    While TCS's 111,000 layoffs were larger in absolute terms, the 18% reduction was less severe than Wipro's 48%.

    Infosys: Managed Decline

    Infosys reduced workforce by 22% (2025-2030):

    • FY2025: 380,000 employees
    • FY2030: 296,000 employees
    • Reduction: 84,000 employees (-22%)

    Infosys benefited from:
    1. Strong consulting heritage: Infosys's consulting practice provided alternative revenue to services decline
    2. Early service mix upgrade: Infosys shifted service mix toward higher-margin consulting/transformation services (2025-2027)
    3. Selective M&A: Infosys acquired specialist consulting firms to supplement internal transformation

    HCL Technologies: Selective Reduction

    HCL Technologies reduced workforce by 24% (2025-2030):

    • FY2025: 220,000 employees
    • FY2030: 167,000 employees
    • Reduction: 53,000 employees (-24%)

    1. Smaller base: HCL was 1/3 TCS size, requiring proportionally larger percentage reductions to match cost pressure
    2. Services-heavy business: Unlike Infosys's consulting, HCL had less alternative revenue stream
    3. Geographic diversity: HCL had stronger presence in Europe/US (higher-margin markets) providing some buffer

    CONCLUSIONS FOR THE WORKFORCE

    The Wipro experience of 2025-2030 represents a cautionary tale for knowledge workers in technology sectors. Key conclusions:

    1. Scale Matters: Being employed by the smallest player in a declining industry creates compounded vulnerability. Wipro's modest size meant it had fewer strategic alternatives during disruption.

    2. Disruption is Rapid: The 18-month compression of 48% workforce reduction demonstrates how quickly labor market dislocation can occur. Workers facing single-digit warning signs should act immediately.

    3. Financial Resilience Critical: Employees with 12+ months emergency savings fared substantially better during 6-11 month job searches. Those without emergency reserves faced deprivation and poor reemployment outcomes.

    4. Skill Diversification Essential: Employees whose skills were heavily concentrated in legacy IT services domains (Java, Oracle, COBOL) faced worse reemployment outcomes. Those with complementary skills (cloud, data, AI basics) transitioned more successfully.

    5. Geographic Concentration Risk: Bangalore's 205,000 IT layoffs created oversupply in a single geography, driving down local wages and making relocation necessary for better opportunities.

    6. Psychological Preparation Important: The psychological impact of layoff exceeded the financial impact for many workers. Those who prepared mentally (maintaining social connections, developing alternative identity beyond job) recovered faster.
