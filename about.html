About The 2030 Intelligence Report

AI Preparedness 318 Memos 67 Countries 142 Companies

The Mission

AI will reshape how billions of people work, invest, govern, learn, and live — and it's happening now, not in some distant future. The problem is that the people who most need to prepare — employees, small business owners, parents, retirees, government leaders, investors — have almost no access to decision-grade intelligence about what's coming.

McKinsey charges $500K+ for one company's AI strategy. The World Economic Forum publishes reports that sit on shelves. Government agencies produce policy papers no citizen reads. LinkedIn influencers post shallow takes.

The 2030 Intelligence Report exists to close that gap. We provide free, personalized, country-specific, audience-specific intelligence memos so that every person — regardless of wealth, position, or geography — can understand what AI disruption means for them and take action before it's too late.

The Method: Memos from the Future

Every article is written as a macro intelligence memo dated June 2030, looking back at how AI transformed economies, companies, and careers between 2025 and 2030. This "retrospective from the future" format lets readers experience two possible outcomes side by side:

The Bear Case: What happens if you do nothing. The cost of waiting, the consequences of passivity, the compounding damage of inaction as competitors, countries, and colleagues move ahead.

The Bull Case: What happens if you act now. The strategic moves, investments, reskilling decisions, and policy changes that position you to thrive — not just survive — the AI revolution.

This isn't abstract futurism. Each memo contains specific data, timelines, strategic frameworks, and decision points tailored to the reader's role and geography.

What's Here

Countries (20): Each country is analyzed through up to 11 lenses — CEO, Investor, Consumer, Government Official, Young Person, Employee, Blue-Collar Worker, Small Business Owner, Educator, Parent, and Retiree. From the United States to Ethiopia, from India to Barbados. Every memo reflects the unique economic structure, labor market, and policy environment of that country.

Companies (142): Major companies across the US, UK, Canada, Australia, India, and Europe, each examined through CEO, Employee, and Investor perspectives. Real companies, real strategic dilemmas, real competitive dynamics.

Sectors (20): The 20 largest sectors by market cap, each analyzed for Incumbent CEOs, Disruptor Founders, Employees, and Customers. From Healthcare to Semiconductors, Banking to Retail.

Who This Is For

If you're a CEO or business leader — your memo shows exactly what happens to companies that wait versus companies that transform. Specific moves, timelines, and competitive dynamics.

If you're an investor — your memo maps which sectors, companies, and geographies win or lose, and the portfolio implications of both scenarios.

If you're an employee — your memo identifies which roles are at risk, which skills gain value, and how to position yourself as AI-augmented rather than AI-replaced.

If you're a government leader — your memo examines tax base erosion, workforce displacement, social safety net requirements, and competitive positioning against other nations.

If you're a young person — your memo cuts through the noise to show which careers, skills, and strategies will matter in a world where AI can do more and more of the work.

If you're a parent, retiree, or small business owner — more memos for you are coming. Everyone deserves a memo written for them.
